Sergei Sobyanin: Big Circle Line became the longest circle metro line in the world

Photo by Vladimir Novikov. Moscow Mayor and Government Press Service
It is 70 kilometers of tracks with 31 stations.

The Big Circle Line (BCL) of the Moscow Metro has become the longest circle metro line in the world. Sergei Sobyanin announced that on his Telegram channel. 

“The longest metro circle line in the world, 70 kilometers long, with 31 stations, was built in record time. It will improve the operation of the Moscow Metro and the entire transport system of the city,” wrote the Moscow Mayor.

Source: Sergei Sobyanin's telegram channel @mos_sobyanin

The BCL is 70 kilometers long and includes 31 stations and 3 train depots. It overtook the Second Circle Line of the Beijing Metro (57 kilometers) and became the largest metro construction project in the world.

The line was built in the shortest time: within 10 years. Only five years have passed since the launch of the first station in 2018 until the completion of construction.

“This is the largest and most challenging project in the history of metro construction. This is the new heart of Moscow's transport system, which brings the MCD, the MCC, and metro lines together into a single transport system and connects new metro lines, shaping the future of the transport system. This is definitely a large and important project, which could never be implemented by just one agency or one industry. All Moscow industries have contributed to this project. Even more: the entire economy and the full potential of the city was used for this project,” Sergei Sobyanin specified at the opening ceremony of the BCL traffic.

The large circle line was built in dense urban areas. Specialists had to shift a lot of transport and engineering services under complex geological conditions. For example, the line’s route has 23 intersections with tunnels of the radial metro lines, 19 intersections with the tracks of the Moscow Central Diameters and the Moscow Central Circle, seven intersections with riverbeds (Moskva, Nishchenka, Tarakanovka, Yauza), including under Moscow Canal floodgate No. 9, passes through the Karamyshevskoye straightening and passes through the bed of the Moskva River twice: on the Terekhovo-Kuntsevskaya sections (the river is more than 100 meters wide there) and Pechatniki-Nagatinsky Zaton (through Perervinsky reach, which is about 450 meters wide).

Much of the work had never been done in Moscow before. For example, during the construction of the Nizhegorodskaya station, the specialists used top-down technology with the underground part built from top to bottom down the earth surface. First, floors and piles-columns are concreted, and then work goes deeper in parallel with the construction of structures on the surface.

The Delovoy Tsentr station was integrated into one of the lower floors of the central core of the Moscow International Business Center. Vibration-damping plates have been installed at the base of the tracks, which absorb vibrations from the train traffic to eliminate the impact of the metro on the strength of high-rise buildings.

148.6 kilometers of tunnels have been built, including 11.6 kilometers of large-diameter double-track tunnels (on the Aminyevskaya-Mnevniki and Tekstilshchiki-Kashirskaya sections). Trains of both directions run in one tunnel instead of two. The double-track tunnels have been built using special tunnel-boring shields with a diameter of 10 meters.

The head of the 10-meter shield weighing 870 tons has been lowered into the assembled shield chamber at the Tekstilshchiki station for the first time in the history of the Moscow metro. The unique operation was performed using a gantry system that can handle a weight of almost 1,200 tons. Prior to this, the shield used to be lowered in parts using multi-ton cranes and mounted in the pit. This system made it possible to reduce the installation time of the tunneling complex by a month.

BCL records

A number of records have been set during the construction of the Big Circle Line.

In just 35 days, Pobeda, a giant 10-meter shield, was launched. It helped construct tunnels in the Klenovy Bulvar-Kashirskaya section.

The longest escalators with a length of 130 meters have been installed at the Maryina Roshcha station.

Thirty-three tunnel-boring shields have been used.

20,000 people have been involved.

The Liliya tunnel shield has covered 500 linear meters in a month while constructing a double-track tunnel on the BCL western section.
