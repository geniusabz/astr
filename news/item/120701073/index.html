Skating rink at VDNKh, Fabergé Museum and Kul Sharif Mosque: Russpass on the most popular routes this winter

Photo by Maxim Denisov. Mos.ru
Russian tourists visited most of the sights during the New Year holidays and weekends between 23 and 26 January.

The digital service, Russpass, told us which tourist destinations were the most popular this winter. Experts studied preferences and behavior of users on the portal and found out what travelers were most interested in.

Russpass offered more than 200 route options in 43 regions of Russia, including Moscow, for winter holidays. Users commonly turned to recommendations of the service between December 25 and January 8 and between February 23 and 26.

Popular New Year destinations

The most popular tourist destinations before the New Year and during the holidays were Moscow, Saint Petersburg, the Republic of Tatarstan, the Stavropol Territory, Nizhny Novgorod, Kaliningrad, Rostov, Yaroslavl, Moscow and Vladimir Regions. Russpass travelers selected and booked flights, hotels and leisure events.

Users started planning their winter holidays in advance: they booked plane tickets more than a month before the departure date, and they booked railway tickets a month before the trip. Most often, tourists chose hotels 20 days before arrival. The peak of booking tickets for cultural events was the first half of December and the middle of the holidays, on January 4 and 5.

For those who chose to spend the New Year holidays in Moscow, sports events and tours for the whole family were in high demand. The top 10 included Moscow Zoo, Moscow Cable Car, skating rink at VDNKh, Mikhail Bulgakov Museum, Pushkin State Museum of Fine Arts, Museum and Historical Complex “Bunker-42”, Ilya Glazunov Gallery, Salvador Dali & Pablo Picasso exhibition, sightseeing tours of the Luzhniki Olympic Complex and river walks.

The second most popular tourist city was Saint Petersburg. Tourists visited the Hermitage, the Fabergé Museum with its permanent exhibitions and the Russian Epos thematic exhibition, Museum “Grand Maket Rossiya” and the Sevkabel Port creative space.

Another popular destination was the Republic of Tatarstan. Tourists visited the Kazan Zoo, the Zambezi River. Africa, and the City Panorama museum. Visitors to the Krasnodar Territory preferred Lake Abrau to many attractions, and tourists who visited the Vladimir Region admired ancient architecture: the Suzdal Kremlin and the Dormition Cathedral.

Popular February weekend destinations

From February 23 to 26, tourists commonly saw around the main sights of Saint Petersburg, Kazan and Nizhny Novgorod, and visited Kaliningrad and its environs.

In Moscow, sites related to science, technology and engineering were selected, including the Moscow Planetarium, Moscow Cable Car, VDNKh and Cosmonautics and Aviation Center, the Digital Art Center Artplay Media, Bunker-703 Museum and the Special Purpose Garage of the FSO of Russia.

In addition to the Hermitage, the Kunstkamera and Nevsky Prospekt, tourists visiting Saint Petersburg were especially interested in Saint Isaac’s Cathedral and Kazan Cathedral, St. Anne’s Church, the Church of the Savior on Spilled Blood and Palace Square. The Kul Sharif Mosque, the Kazan Center with an observation deck on Kazan, the Kazan Kremlin and walking along the Kremlin Embankment and Bauman Street appealed to visitors to Kazan.

This year, Maslenitsa celebration coincided with the long February weekend, which inspired many travelers to participate in special programs in different regions of Russia.

Russpass offered its users 15 special routes around the Moscow Maslenitsa festival in the Russian capital and holiday programs in other cities. Tourists chose walking tours in Moscow, Tula, Kostroma, Yaroslavl, Kazan and Rostov.

Travelers took care of booking train tickets for the long weekend about 20 days before the date of the trip, and they chose tours and other entertainment activity right before the holidays: the activity peak was recorded on February 20. On this day, they typically viewed and added attractions and events in museums of different cities to favorites and bought tickets.
