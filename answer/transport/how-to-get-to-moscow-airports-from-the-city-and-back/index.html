1.How many airports are there in Moscow?

There are four airports in Moscow — Sheremetyevo, Domodedovo, Vnukovo and Zhukovsky.


Sheremetyevo is the base airport of the largest Russian airline — Aeroflot. Sheremetyevo is located northwest of Moscow. There are several ways to get to it from Moscow and back.


Domodedovo is the main Russian airport for a major Russian airline — S7 Airlines. It is located south of Moscow. There are several ways to get there from Moscow and to the city.


Vnukovo is the base airport for the Russian low-cost airline Pobeda. It is located outside the Moscow Ring Road to the southwest of the city. There are several ways to get to it from Moscow and back.


Zhukovsky is a new airport in Moscow. It is located to the east of Moscow. There are several ways to get to it from Moscow and back.

2.How to get from Moscow to Sheremetyevo and back?

You can take an Aeroexpress train, bus, or taxi to get from Moscow to Sheremetyevo and back. You can use car sharing services or rent a car, but you should not forget that parking in the city center is paid, and traffic jams will take a lot of time.


Please note! You can pay for your trip (Aeroexpress, bus, taxi) by card or in cash in rubles. No cash other than rubles (US dollars, euros, etc.) is accepted for payment. You can withdraw cash rubles at ATMs at the airport. There are also cash exchange booths in the airport, but changing money in the city is usually more profitable.


Aeroexpress

Aeroexpress is the fastest way to get to the city center from the airport and back. The fare is about six dollars per person.


Aeroexpress trains run between Odintsovo, a town around Moscow, and Sheremetyevo Airport from early morning until late at night. Trains goes with several stops from Odintsovo to Belorussky railway station (metro station Belorusskaya of the Ring Line). In addition, you can take the Aeroexpress train at the Okruzhnaya MCD-1 station and at Savelovsky Railway Station. The fare from any of these stations to the airport is the same. Visit the Aeroexpress website to find more about the schedule, fares and payment methods.


Buses and minibuses

Taking a bus or minibus is the cheapest way to get from the airport to the city or back. The travel time depends on the time of day and day of the week, as the entrances to the city during rush hour are very busy. 


Special Aeroexpress buses run between Sheremetyevo Airport and Khovrino metro station (the upper terminus of the Green Line). The fare is about three dollars (cards are also accepted for payment). Buses arrive each 20 minutes, and the estimated travel time is also 20 minutes. Visit the airport's website to learn more about express buses.


Buses also go from Sheremetyevo to the metro stations Planernaya (the upper terminus of the purple line), Belomorskaya and Rechnoy Vokzal (the second and third top stations of the green line). By night bus, one can get from the airport to the metro stations Kitai-gorod or Voikovskaya (the station of the purple and orange lines in the center of Moscow and the fifth station on the green line, respectively). In addition, Sheremetyevo can be reached by bus from the cities of Khimki and Lobnya near Moscow. The fare is about one dollar.


You can find out more about the routes and departure times on the airport's website.


An interactive map in the Moscow Metro mobile app would help to set your route and calculate the time of your trip. The app is available in Russian and English.


Taxi

You can get to the airport from the city or back by taxi. The trip will cost at least 20-40 dollars. The travel time depends on the time of day and day of the week, as the entrances to the city during rush hour are very busy.


There are many taxi services in Moscow. Yandex.Taxi, Uber are among the most popular ones. Application for these services are available at App Store (Yandex.Taxi, Uber) and Google Play (Yandex.Taxi, Uber).


Sheremetyevo Airport has a partner taxi service. Visit the airport's website to find out more about the location of the booking counters and fares.


Car sharing and car rental

You can also use a car sharing service or rent a car to get from the city to the airport or back. But not all companies that provide carsharing and car rental services in Moscow operate at airports. For more information about carsharing in Sheremetyevo, please visit the airport's website in the Carsharing section. The information about car rental can be found in the Car Rental section.

6.How can people with limited mobility get from Moscow to airports and back?

If you need assistance for travel on metro, Moscow Central Circle (MCC) or Moscow Central Diameter (MCD), you can use the services of the Passenger Mobility Support Center (PMSC) of the Moscow Metro. 


PMSC employees provide assistance to people with limited mobility: those with hearing, vision, and musculoskeletal disorders, the elderly, large families, parents with children under seven years of age, and others, as well as organized groups of passengers, including children (children under 11 years old).


Support services are provided daily from 05:30 to 01:00.


To get travel assistance on the metro, MCC or MCD you need to apply at least 24 hours before your planned trip (for organized groups applications should be submitted at least 10 days in advance).


You can submit applications:

  • in the Moscow Metro mobile app;
  • on the site;
  • by calling the Moscow Metro PMSC hotline: 8 (495) 622-73-41 or 8 (800) 250-73-41;
  • by sending SMS to 8 (926) 250-47-80.


Hotline operators speak Russian and English. 


You can also use the Personal Assistant service to have passengers with limited mobility accompanied on the Aeroexpress trains. The service includes a meeting at the Aeroexpress terminal, assistance with buying an Aeroexpress ticket, boarding a car and placing luggage.


The service is available for the following categories of people with limited mobility:

  • visually and/or hearing challenged people;
  • passengers with musculoskeletal disorders;
  • wheelchair users;
  • disabled children;
  • elderly people;
  • passengers with temporary limited mobility (including pregnant women);
  • passengers with children.


You can order the Personal Assistant service:

  • at the information desks in the Aeroexpress terminals and at train stations; 
  • by phone: 8 (800) 700-33-77 (for calls from the regions of Russia and from mobile phones), 8 (495) 663-84-10 (for calls from Moscow);
  • on the website.


Hotline operators speak Russian and English.


All services are provided free of charge, you will only need to pay for the ticket price in the metro and Aeroexpress.


Airports also provide support services for people with limited mobility. You can learn more about the service on the websites of airports (Sheremetyevo, Domodedovo, Vnukovo, Zhukovsky).
